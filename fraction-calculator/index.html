    How fraction arithmetic works

    Every fraction operation comes down to two ideas: a common denominator for adding and subtracting, and simplifying at the end by dividing out the greatest common divisor. Multiplying is the friendly one — just multiply across the top and across the bottom. Dividing is the same, after you flip the second fraction (its reciprocal). Master those and fractions stop being scary.

    • Add / subtract: put both over the same denominator, combine the numerators, simplify.
    • Multiply: numerator × numerator over denominator × denominator, then simplify.
    • Divide: keep the first, flip the second, multiply — "keep, change, flip".
    • Mixed numbers: convert to an improper fraction first (2 1/3 → 7/3), then proceed.

    Frequently asked questions

    How do I add fractions with different denominators?

    Rewrite both over a common denominator, add the numerators, then simplify. 1/3 + 1/4 → 4/12 + 3/12 = 7/12.

    How do I multiply and divide fractions?

    Multiply across (2/3 × 4/5 = 8/15). To divide, flip the second and multiply (2/3 ÷ 4/5 = 2/3 × 5/4 = 5/6).

    How do I simplify a fraction?

    Divide top and bottom by their greatest common divisor. 8/12 ÷ 4/4 = 2/3.

    How do I turn an improper fraction into a mixed number?

    Divide numerator by denominator: 7/3 = 2 remainder 1 = 2 1/3.
