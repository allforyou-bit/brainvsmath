    The four steps, every time

    Long division looks intimidating but it is just one short loop repeated: Divide, Multiply, Subtract, Bring down (some remember it as "Does McDonald's Sell Burgers?"). You work left to right across the dividend, and each pass produces one digit of the answer.

    1. Divide: how many times does the divisor fit into the current number?
    2. Multiply: that quotient digit × the divisor.
    3. Subtract: take it away to find what's left.
    4. Bring down: pull down the next digit and repeat. Whatever remains at the end is the remainder.

    Always check: quotient × divisor + remainder should equal the number you started with.

    Frequently asked questions

    What are the steps of long division?

    Divide, multiply, subtract, bring down — repeated until no digits are left. Anything remaining is the remainder.

    How do I write a remainder?

    After the last subtraction, write what's left with an R: 47 ÷ 5 = 9 R 2 (9×5 = 45, 47−45 = 2).

    How should I practice?

    A few a day, easy → hard. Check each answer by multiplying back. This page gives you unlimited fresh problems and a printable set of 12.
